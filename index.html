Working frame

60 percent traditional code and database work. 30 percent rule-based logic. 10 percent actual AI.

You stood up the Rapid Innovation Accelerator with Lab22c and the DoD Office of Small Business Programs in October 2024, added the Miami cohort on top of a Dearborn APEX footprint that already serves Wayne County, and sit between a Maryland headquarters, a Michigan program office, and a growing Florida partnership that includes FIU's SBDC and Miami Dade College. That is five or more delivery partners coordinating intake, counseling, and outcome reporting for small firms trying to enter the defense industrial base. Most of the coordination work is document work, and most of the document work is invisible until a funder asks for a report.

Eduba's working frame is 60/30/10. Roughly 60 percent of what a nonprofit like MoveAmerica burns staff hours on is traditional code and database work: shared intake, structured consortia artifacts, a reviewable outcome schema. Another 30 percent is rule-based logic: who routes to whom, which certifications open which set-asides, when a capability statement triggers a counselor handoff. Only about 10 percent is actual AI work: pre-drafting a capability statement from structured intake, surfacing bid-match patterns across cohorts, compressing consortia meeting minutes into reviewable artifacts. Teams that skip this distinction spend budget putting large language models where a Postgres schema would produce better, cheaper, and more auditable results. Our job is to help you figure out which layer each piece of the RIA belongs on before you scale to cohort two.

Closest case study

160hrs collapsed to <5min

VigilOre

A small organization running a regulated, document-heavy workflow. We designed and delivered a multi-agent compliance system that collapsed 160 hours of manual compliance review to under five minutes, structured as a scoped, tiered engagement. The same pattern applies to consortia management and APEX counseling, where the unit of work is a document that has to be produced, reviewed, cited, and archived against a federal reporting requirement.

The methodology behind it

If the structure interests you, the published methodology behind it is Interpretable Context Methodology (ICM), submitted to ACM TiiS: agent context organized as a layered filesystem, with measurable interpretability and reproducibility gains. It is the spine of how we would design the RIA's shared intake and outcome layer. Repository: github.com/RinDig/Interpretable-Context-Methodology-ICM-.
